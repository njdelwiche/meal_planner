Baked Cauliflower with Chickpeas

Source
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 5 big cloves of garlic, smashed with the flat of a knife
  • 1/4 - 1/2 teaspoon chili flakes
  • 1 can tomato paste (6 ounces)
  • 1 tablespoon harissa
  • 1 bag cauliflower rice (12 ounces)
  • 3 cups cooked chickpeas
  • 8 ounces feta, crumbled
  • 1/2 cup sun dried tomatoes, julienned
Step 1

Heat oven to 375 F.

Step 2

Add the olive oil to a large oven-safe skillet, heat over medium flame. When the oil is hot, add in the garlic cloves and chili flakes.

Step 3

Once you’ve cooked the garlic till it’s light golden brown on both sides, add in the tomato paste. Cook until it’s brick red, adding in the harissa a few minutes before you’re there.

Step 4

Add in the cauliflower rice and some water — maybe 1 cup. You want it to be saucy but not too loose.

Step 5

Add in the chickpeas and adjust water as necessary — again, you’re going for saucy, and remember that some of the water will evaporate when you bake it.

Step 6

Stir in the sun dried tomatoes and crumbled feta and pop the whole thing in the oven. Bake for 20-30 minutes — I’m not sure it matters how long you bake it for? You just want time for the chickpeas to get a little more tender and everything to heat up. That’s it! If you have a pan and a stocked pantry, you can make it for dinner tonight.
